# Start here

This Google Drive folder is your AI Operating System. It is the one place your AI reads to know who you are, how you work, what tools it can reach, and what you have decided. It works the same way whether you run it through Claude, Gemini, or ChatGPT. The folder is the source of truth. The AI is the front door.

## Three parts working together

- **This folder (the file system).** What the AI knows about you. You and the AI both add to it over time.
- **The project, gem, or custom GPT (the brain).** A workspace in your AI that reads this folder on every chat, so you never re-explain yourself.
- **The skills (the hands).** Small reusable procedures the AI runs the same way every time. On Claude you upload them as Skills. On Gemini they live in your Gem, on ChatGPT in your Custom GPT.

## Paths

The OS is split by area of life so the AI keeps them straight. You only set up the ones you use.

- `business/` — the company or practice you run.
- `personal/` — your own life: family, health, money, the things you manage outside work.
- `work/` — a job you hold, a role inside someone else's company.

Each path holds the same shape:

- `overview.md` — the living picture of that area. Current state, the people, the processes. Kept up to date. This is the file the AI leans on most.
- `references/` — frameworks, checklists, reusable notes for that area.
- `decisions/` — a dated record of decisions and changes. Append-only. Nothing is ever overwritten.

## Shared across every path

- `me.md` — who you are and how you want the AI to work with you. Read first, every time.
- `_shared/connections.md` — the tools the AI can reach and what each is for.
- `_shared/platforms.md` — how the OS adapts to Claude, Gemini, or ChatGPT.
- `_shared/frameworks.md` — the thinking the OS runs on.
- `_shared/skills/INDEX.md` — the skills you have and what each does.
- `inbox/` — fast capture. Drop a thought here in one line and let the weekly pass sort it into the right path.

## How to begin

You do not fill any of this in cold. In your AI project, say **"onboard me"** (Claude), or paste the onboarding prompt into your Gem or Custom GPT. It asks which platform you use and which paths you want, then it interviews you one question at a time and writes your files from your answers. It shows you each one before saving.

After that, the rhythm is small and weekly: log decisions as they happen, run the audit to see what is thin or leaking, and level up one manual task into a skill.
